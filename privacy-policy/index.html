5. Cookies

What Are Cookies

A cookie is a small file that contains a string of characters and is sent to your computer when you visit a website. When you return to the site, the cookie allows it to recognize your browser. Cookies can store user preferences and other information.

Cookies set by Alex AI Insurtech are referred to as "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than us are known as "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies enable the provision of features or functionalities from third parties on or through the website (e.g., advertising, interactive content, and analytics). The entities that place these third-party cookies can recognize your computer both when it visits our website and when it accesses certain other websites.

Why We Use Cookies

We utilize both first-party and third-party cookies for various reasons. Some cookies are necessary for technical purposes to ensure our website operates correctly, and we refer to these as "essential" or "strictly necessary" cookies. Other cookies help us track and target user interests to enhance the overall experience on our website. For instance, Alex AI Insurtech monitors the website and pages you visit within our platform to identify which sections are most popular or frequently accessed. This information is used to deliver tailored content and promotions within the Alex AI Insurtech website to users whose behavior indicates interest in specific topics. Third parties may also serve cookies through our website for advertising, analytics, and other functions, as detailed below.

Types of Cookies We Use and How We Use Them

The specific types of cookies utilized on our website and their purposes are as follows:

  • Essential Website Cookies: These cookies are crucial for providing you with services available on our website. Since they are strictly necessary for delivering the website, you cannot opt out of them. However, you can block or delete these cookies by adjusting your browser settings as explained below.
  • Performance and Functionality Cookies: These cookies enhance the performance and functionality of our website but are not essential for its use. Without these cookies, certain features may become unavailable.
  • Analytics and Customization Cookies: These cookies gather information that is used in aggregate form to help us understand how our website is utilized or to assess the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns, as well as to customize our website according to your preferences.
  • Advertising Cookies: These cookies aim to make advertising messages more relevant to you. They perform functions such as preventing the same ad from appearing repeatedly, ensuring that ads are displayed correctly for advertisers, and, in some cases, selecting advertisements based on your interests.
  • Social Networking Cookies: These cookies allow you to share pages and content that you find interesting on our website through third-party social networks and other websites. Additionally, these cookies may also be used for advertising purposes.

How Can You Control Cookies?

You have the right to choose whether to accept or decline cookies. You can manage your cookie preferences by using the provided opt-out links below. Additionally, you can adjust your web browser settings to either accept or reject cookies. Note that if you choose to disable cookies, you may still access our website, but some features or sections might be restricted.

The methods for refusing cookies vary by browser, so consult your browser‚Äôs help menu for further guidance. Many advertising networks also offer an option to opt out of targeted ads. To learn more, please visit http://www.aboutads.info/choices/ or http://www.youronlinechoices.com. You can opt out by visiting http://preferences.truste.com/.

Please be aware that opting out will not prevent you from seeing ads altogether. You will still receive general advertisements.

Other Tracking Technologies, Such as Web Beacons

Cookies are not the only tools we use to identify or monitor visitors to our website. We also use a software technology called clear gifs (also known as Web Beacons or Web Bugs) to help us assess the effectiveness of the website and our subscription service by showing us which content resonates with users. Clear gifs are small graphics with unique identifiers, functioning similarly to cookies, and are used to monitor the online movements of website visitors. Unlike cookies, which are saved on the hard drive of your device, clear gifs are embedded invisibly on web pages or in emails, and are typically no larger than the period at the end of this sentence. We use these clear gifs or pixels in HTML-based emails to track which emails have been opened. This enables us to measure the impact of our communications and the effectiveness of our marketing efforts. We link the information collected through clear gifs in emails to our customers‚Äô Personal Information.
